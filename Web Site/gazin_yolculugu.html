Gas Journey: Field Architecture & SCADA

Offshore Production Process & Bottlenecks from Reservoir to the National Grid

Production Phases

1. Downhole Dynamics

Gas enters the wellbore driven by reservoir pressure (Pr). It climbs the production tubing, overcoming hydrostatic and friction resistance, and safely passes the SSSV.

2. Subsea Architecture

Reaching the seabed, the gas experiences Joule-Thomson cooling as it flows through the Subsea Tree choke. MEG is injected to prevent hydrates. It merges with other wells at the Manifold and ascends via the SCR Riser.

3. Topside Facility (FPU)

Multiphase fluid enters the Separator and undergoes gravity separation. Gas is dehydrated via TEG and pressurized by the Export Compressor. Excess gas is safely flared.

4. Export & Onshore Reception

The dry gas travels through the 170 km subsea export pipeline. Arriving at the Filyos Facility, it enters the Slug Catcher, undergoes final filtration, and is measured by Fiscal Meters before grid delivery.
