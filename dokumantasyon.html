System Documentation & References

All simulators in this platform are developed based on the fundamental mathematical and physical models found in petroleum and natural gas engineering literature.

📘 Well & Field Architecture (Equipment Atlas)

Covers the entire lifecycle of offshore and onshore wells, from drilling to surface facilities. Visualizes BOP mechanics, Subsea Tree operations, and the Fail-Safe Closed logic of critical systems.

Reference Golan, M. & Whitson, C. H. (1991). Well Performance. (Chapter: Wellbore & Surface Completion Equipment)

📊 Reservoir & Advanced Analysis (PVT, PTA, Coning)

PVT Phase Envelope: Analyzes "Retrograde Condensation" phenomena in gas condensate systems.
Well Testing (PTA): Utilizes Bourdet's derivative curve on Log-Log plots for the detection of wellbore storage (WBS), radial flow, and reservoir boundaries (Faults, Aquifers, Dual Porosity).
Water Coning: Simulates the physics of formation water breakthrough caused by exceeding the critical flow rate.

Reference Economides, M. J. et al. (2012). Petroleum Production Systems. (Chapter: Reservoir Deliverability & Well Testing)

⚠️ Drilling & Downhole (Kick, Skin, Acidizing)

Well Control (Kick): Dynamics of formation gas influx (Boyle's Law) and the Driller's Method involving pumping Kill Weight Mud to secure the well.
Matrix Acidizing: Removing formation damage (Skin) while staying below the Fracture Pressure limit. Illustrates the leak-off problem if the pressure threshold is exceeded.

Ref 1Guo, B. et al. (2007). Petroleum Production Engineering. (Chapter: Well Completion)
Ref 2Economides, M. J. Reservoir Stimulation. (Chapter: Matrix Acidizing)

📈 Production & System Analysis (Nodal, Flow, J-T)

Nodal Analysis: Determining the optimal well production rate via the intersection of Inflow Performance Relationship (IPR) and Tubing Performance Relationship (TPR/VLP).
Joule-Thomson (Choke): The severe expansion and temperature drop (Isenthalpic process) experienced by gas passing through a restriction.
Flow Regimes (Liquid Loading): Bubble, Slug, Churn, and Annular Mist regimes in vertical pipes, emphasizing the critical gas velocity required to lift liquid droplets (Turner's Criterion).

Main RefBeggs, H. D. (2003). Production Optimization Using NODAL™ Analysis.

🚀 Artificial Lift (Gas Lift, ESP)

Gas Lift: Injecting gas into the annulus to aerate the fluid column in the tubing, thereby reducing hydrostatic pressure. Highlights the friction penalty of over-injection.
ESP (Electric Submersible Pump): Using a VFD (Variable Frequency Drive) to pressurize fluids through centrifugal pump stages, mapped via Pump Head vs. Flow Rate affinity laws.

Reference Guo, B. et al. (2007). Petroleum Production Engineering. (Chapter: Artificial Lift Methods)

🛢️ Surface & Flow Assurance (Separator, Hydrates, Pigging)

3-Phase Separator: Gravitational separation based on Stokes' Law, focusing on Residence Time and the risk of Carry-over.
Hydrate Phase Diagram: Prevention of gas hydrates (ice plugs) forming under high pressure and low seabed temperatures using MEG/Thermodynamic inhibitors.
Pigging Operations: Sweeping accumulated liquids (Liquid Holdup) from subsea flowline bathymetric dips into the onshore Slug Catcher.

FacilitiesGuo, B. et al. (2007). Petroleum Production Engineering. (Chapter: Surface Facilities)
